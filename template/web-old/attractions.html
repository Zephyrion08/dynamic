Manakamana Temple

Manakamana Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to goddess Bhagwati, an incarnation of Parvati and it is situated in the village of Manakamana in Gorkha District, Gandaki Province, Nepal founded by Arman Rayamajhi in 1678

Gorakhnath Siddha Cave

It is another site that all should visit is the Gorakhnath Gufa (cave). Legend has it that the sage Gorakhnath came here to meditate and perform his prayers. It is said that during this time the king of Gorkha kingdom Prithivi Narayan Shah came to pay his respects to the sage Gorakhnath.

Bakreshwor Mahadev

Another popular holy site in Manakamana is the temple of ‘Bakreshwor Mahadev’ which lies only 1.5 km towards the north of the Manakamana temple. It is believed that while the lord Shiva walked carrying the dead body of his beloved wife Sati, her body parts fell on the paths that he took and upon reaching this point in Nepal, the tongue of Sati fell off.
